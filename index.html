About me

I like problems where the data is messy, the hardware is real, and the system has to work anyway.

I'm a software engineer working across backend systems and computer vision, currently at Fraunhofer IEM building airport-automation software with Paderborn-Lippstadt Airport. I'm developing optimization algorithms for automated luggage packing (my M.Sc. thesis), and previously built point-cloud alignment and aircraft localisation for FastGate — accurate to 5–10 cm, and demoed at Fraport in Frankfurt.

Before Germany, I spent two years at Persistent Systems running production database migration and replication across SQL, MySQL, Oracle and PostgreSQL.

Finishing my M.Sc. in Computer Science at Paderborn University. Open to backend, computer-vision and platform engineering roles across the EU.
